# BlackHoleSimulations
Here I simulate the effect of random particles around black holes on the basis of Gravity models and types. 
When simulating black holes and the movement of matter around them, astronomers and physicists choose a gravity model based on a trade-off between computational speed and physical accuracy.
These gravity models generally fall into three main categories: Newtonian (Classical), Pseudo-Newtonian (Approximated Relativity), and Fully Relativistic (General Relativity).
1] Newtonian (Classical)
2] Pseudo-Newtonian (Approximated Relativity) :- Paczyński-Wiita Potential & Artemova-Novikov-Thorne Potential.
3] Fully Relativistic (General Relativity):- Schwarzschild Metric, Kerr Metric & Numerical Relativity (Full Einstein Field Equations).
